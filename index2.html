Introduction

Electrical tools are required for repairs or improvement projects around the house. For most residential electrical projects, you'll use primarily basic hand tools you already own, such as a hammer, tape measure, laser level, flathead, and Phillips screwdrivers. Some specialty electrical tools like voltage testers, wire strippers are also used.

1.Non-Contact Voltage Tester

Perhaps the most crucial specialty electrical tool you can own is a voltage tester.
A voltage tester is used for a quick safety check to ensure no current is flowing
to an electrical wire or device before you start working on it. Powered by
batteries, non-contact voltage testers are the simplest and safest
testers because they can detect electricity near an outlet slot or wire.

3.Hammer

A hammer is used to secure electrical boxes equipped with nail-on brackets to wall studs
and other framing members in a home. You’ll also need one to drive wire staples when
anchoring new electrical cables to framing members.

4.Voltmeter or Multimeter

A voltmeter reads voltage levels and verifies that circuits are “live” or off. Unlike a circuit tester

5.Phillips Screwdrivers

Electricians keep screwdrivers with them at all times for removing and installing cover plates,screws,outlets
switches and many other devices.

6.Straight-Blade Screwdrivers

As with Phillips screwdrivers, you will likely need more than one size of straight-blade screwdriver. If you have
to choose just one, pick a medium blade; it will suit most projects.

7.Electric Screwdriver

An electric screwdriver is a portable device that can screw and unscrew using a battery or corded power.
